2025–2026 Competitive Events Guidelines

Topic: Byte-Sized Business Boost — High School Division

Design and present a standalone program that helps users discover and support local small businesses.

Event Overview

  • Division: High School — Team of 1–3 members
  • Event type: Presentation with a topic
  • Focus: technical skills, logic, creativity

Topic Requirements

  • Sort businesses by category (food, retail, services)
  • Allow reviews/ratings and sort by them
  • Save/bookmark favorites
  • Display special deals/coupons
  • Include verification to prevent bot activity

Eligibility & Logistics

  • Membership, registration, and hotel requirements apply
  • State and district entry limits — check local rules
  • Team members must be from same chapter

Presentation Format

  • Two phases: Preliminary & Final
  • Set-up: 3 minutes | Presentation: 7 minutes | Q&A: 3 minutes
  • Time segments are exclusive — do not shift time

Technology Guidelines

  • Program must run standalone with no programming errors
  • Use no more than three personal devices during presentation
  • Internet provided but unreliable — prepare an offline backup
  • Projectors/screens availability depends on round; bring adapters

Judging & Scoring Highlights

  • Judges use a rating sheet covering code quality, UX, functionality
  • Key areas: language selection, comments, modular design, validation
  • Prelim scores determine finalists; final scores determine awards

Rating Sheet: What To Emphasize

  • Explain language choice with industry terms
  • Show modular code, clear comments, and naming conventions
  • Demonstrate full requirements and intelligent features (filters, Q&A)
  • Provide input validation and meaningful error handling

Pre-Presentation Checklist

  • Run the app offline: no runtime errors, no console warnings
  • Prepare readme, source code, and documentation for judges
  • Bring any adapters needed for HDMI (if required for final round)
  • Practice timing: 3min set-up, 7min demo, 3min Q&A

Presentation Tips

  • All team members should participate
  • Demonstrate features; avoid reading slides verbatim
  • Answer questions clearly and confidently
  • Have a backup plan for internet or device failure

Penalties & Rules

  • Late arrival or dress code violations may incur penalty points
  • Unauthorized recording is prohibited
  • Follow Competitive Events Policy & Honor Code

Contact & References

Check district/state/NLC resources for event-specific deadlines and instructions.

Include citations for any third-party materials used in your documentation.
